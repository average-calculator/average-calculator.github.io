The Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) calculator is a valuable tool used to determine the average annual growth rate of an investment over a specific period. It is particularly useful for assessing the performance of long-term investments such as stocks, mutual funds, and retirement accounts. If you're wondering how to calculate compound annual growth rate, this tool simplifies the process.

The formula for calculating CAGR is:

CAGR = ((Final Value / Initial Value)^(1 / Number of Years)) - 1

Where:

  • Final Value: The ending balance or value of the investment.
  • Initial Value: The starting balance or value of the investment.
  • Number of Years: The total number of years over which the investment has grown.

While this calculator provides an easy way to find the CAGR, many users also want to know how do i calculate compound annual growth rate in excel or how do you calculate compound annual growth rate in excel. You can easily do this using the POWER function in Excel with the same formula: `((Final Value / Initial Value)^(1 / Number of Years)) - 1`.
